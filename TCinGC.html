Context.

While most of the websites I build are done from scratch (HTML, CSS, JS, etc framework), this past semester I had the opportunity to collaborate with the Carnegie Mellon University Information Systems department again to revamp and manage updates to the TCinGC website with Professor Julia Poepping and Professor Joe Mertz.

TCinGC is a program at Carnegie Mellon University that connects students with foreign organizations in developing countries and allows them to use their knowledge of technology to perform an internship abroad while aiding people who actually need it.

As a web assistent, my job involved updating information, creating new pages and getting extremely familiar with the Wix interface. While doing this project, I was impressed by the amount of features Wix offered and allowed users to utilize. Much of it is extremely intuitive even to someone without webdev experience, yet more complex features certainly were easier to learn with previous experience.

With this project, I was very grateful that I was able to learn how to implement a Wix Website, especially one for such a meaningful program.
