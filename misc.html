In a lot of my projects, especially for classes I've taken at University, I oftentimes do not end up coding an entire website, but still get valuable experience from the process of creating wireframes and designs.

CMU English
(web assistent)

Over this past semester I've been working with the CMU English Website to do maintenance and site changes on the official Carnegie Mellon Univeristy English Website.

All Carnegie Mellon Univeristy Sites are made with a custom CMS (Content Management System), and as such I had to learn how to use this service in order to create this site. I've progressed from cosmetic changes such as updating office numbers and phone numbers to creating whole new pages and tags/categories for backend organization. I've also worked with updating permissions for different users, gaining more experience with backend management.

With this project, I'm consistently learning new things about web development which I haven't previously encountered, and I've learned how to think on the spot, even when working with wholely new systems for development.
